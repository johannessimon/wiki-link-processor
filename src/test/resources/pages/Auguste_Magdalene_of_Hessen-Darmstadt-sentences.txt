Auguste Magdalene of Hesse-Darmstadt (6 March 1657, Darmstadt &ndash; 1 September 1674, ibid.) was a German noblewoman and poet.
Auguste Magdalene was a daughter of Count Louis VI of Hesse-Darmstadt (1630–1678) from his marriage to Maria Elisabeth of Holstein-Gottorp (1634–1665), a daughter of Duke Frederick III of Holstein-Gottorp.
Like her father and her sister Magdalene Sibylle, she was active as a writer.
She translated the Psalms of David into German verse and wrote a poetry collection entitled The door to German poetry.
She died only 17 years old and was buried in the City Church in Darmstadt.
