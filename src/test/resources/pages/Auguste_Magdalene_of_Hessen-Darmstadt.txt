'''Auguste Magdalene of Hesse-Darmstadt''' (6 March 1657, [[Darmstadt]] &ndash; 1 September 1674, ibid.) was a German noblewoman and poet.

== Life ==
Auguste Magdalene was a daughter of Count [[Louis VI, Landgrave of Hesse-Darmstadt|Louis VI of Hesse-Darmstadt]] (1630–1678) from his marriage to [[Maria Elisabeth of Holstein-Gottorp]] (1634–1665), a daughter of Duke [[Frederick III, Duke of Holstein-Gottorp|Frederick III of Holstein-Gottorp]].

Like her father and her sister [[Magdalena Sibylla of Hesse-Darmstadt|Magdalene Sibylle]], she was active as a writer.  She translated the Psalms of David into German verse and wrote a poetry collection entitled ''The door to German poetry''. She died only 17 years old and was buried in the City Church in [[Darmstadt]].
